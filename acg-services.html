AI speed amplifies hidden weaknesses. Code that would have taken a team a quarter to write now lands in a week — and so do its flaws. Hallucinated APIs, shallow tests, over-permissioned agents, structural debt, and silent security regressions all ship at the same velocity as the features that look impressive in a demo. The faster a codebase grows under AI assistance, the more it needs an independent party whose only job is to see what the speed conceals.

The Guild offers that party. Our services are arranged as a ladder, because the right level of scrutiny depends on where you are. You can step on at any rung. You can climb as far as your system warrants. We sell no tooling, so every recommendation is the one we would give a friend.

The Ladder

Four rungs, each building on the last. Start where you need to start; climb as far as your stakes require.

Rung 1 — Entry

Diagnostic

A short, focused conversation and surface assessment of your AI-assisted development practice. We look at how AI is being used, where the obvious risks sit, and whether deeper review is warranted. The diagnostic is the cheapest way to find out whether you have a problem worth solving — and it ends with an honest answer, not a sales pitch.

Rung 2 — Inspection

Audit Services

Independent, candid review of an AI-assisted codebase: hallucination detection, security vulnerabilities, test integrity, dependency and license risk, architecture coherence, data handling, error handling, and agent configuration. The audit tells you what your codebase actually contains — not what the tooling reports it contains.

Rung 3 — Remediation

Optimization Services

Once the audit has surfaced what is broken, bloated, or wasteful, optimization removes it. We reduce token waste, eliminate dead weight, tighten structure, and lower the ongoing cost of running and maintaining an AI-assisted codebase. The audit finds the problems; optimization eliminates the conditions that create them.

Rung 4 — Assurance

Continuous Security Assurance

A standing, vendor-neutral relationship that verifies your AI-assisted code and agent behaviour continue to meet the open security canon — OWASP Agentic Top 10, OWASP LLM Top 10, SAFE-MCP, NIST AI Agent Standards, and applicable regulatory regimes — on the cadence your work actually moves at. Point-in-time audits expire; continuous assurance does not.

The Impartial Layer That Watches the Watchers

Guild Ambition

The market is filling with instruments. Security fabrics, governance platforms, evaluation harnesses, agent monitors — each one a watcher, each one sold by a vendor with a roadmap to protect and a story to tell about its own coverage. Useful tools. But a tool cannot grade itself, and a vendor cannot grade its competitors.

The Guild's ambition is to become the impartial layer that watches the watchers.

We are the party with nothing to sell except the accuracy of our assessment. We verify whether your tooling is configured to enforce the standards it claims to enforce. We check whether your audits cover the surface that actually matters. We tell you when a dashboard is reassuring and wrong. And because the standards evolve and your system evolves, we do it on a rhythm that keeps up with both.

Independence is not a marketing posture. It is a structural commitment: no platform, no fabric, no proprietary taxonomy, no referral fees. Just craft-grounded human judgment, applied honestly, against open standards the whole industry can read.

Where To Step On

The ladder is sequential by design, but you do not have to start at the bottom.

Not sure if you have a problem?

Start with the Diagnostic. It is the lowest-cost, lowest-commitment way to find out whether deeper review is warranted.

Shipping AI-assisted code into production?

Start with an Audit. Independent scrutiny before launch is far cheaper than after.

Already audited — now what?

Move to Optimization. The findings are only valuable once they are remediated.

Running agents in production at scale?

Engage Continuous Security Assurance. Point-in-time review cannot keep up with agentic systems.
